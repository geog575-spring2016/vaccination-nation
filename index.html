Vacination Nation

The volatility of the debate over childhood vaccination has made the public curious, frustrated, and even anxious about the actual impact of vaccination policy on public health.

Lacking a general overview of childhood vaccination policy and exemptions in the United States, concerned citizens have trouble gauging whether the cries of government overreach from anti-vaccination advocates have merit. Similarly, without a trustworthy visualization of the patterns of vaccine-preventable childhood illness outbreaks in the United States over time, members of the public are unable to assess the claims of public health experts warning about the risks of declining vaccine coverage. Indeed, without an easily navigable representation of vaccine coverage rates, our readers are unable to determine whether vaccination rates have actually even declined or whether there might be any discernible pattern to such a decline.
